Calculation Algorithms

In principle you have to distinct between two different calculation algorithms at the (quasi) stationary calculation:

Solver for general configurations

General Configurations are models without any restrictions in the build-up of the model.The calculation is done without special knowledge about the drive train. This guarantees the universal use also at complex systems without knowing the plain way from the engine to the wheels (independent of the topology).

Solver for special configurations

Especially for the time efficient stationary and quasi-stationary calculation a technique for the solving of the equations of motion is used, which requires the knowledge about the topology and together with this requires restrictions in modeling. The loss in freedom of modeling leads in this case to a win in calculation time.

In the following the used principles for the solving of the equations of motion are described. In addition the restrictions and the advantages of both techniques should be shown.

In general all component specific calculations are done in the respective modules. The values which are determined in the modules are combined together to an equation system with the smallest possible number of degrees of freedom. The integration for this reduced system can be done independent of the external modules in the internal equation solver.

When building up the equation system an automatic reduction of the masses belonging to a connection is done, to decrease the number of degrees of freedom.

For every component a number of describing equations is available. During the calculation the internal connection can change its status between dynamic and kinematic coupling. For each calculation step the lock conditions of the connections are checked and if necessary the equation system has to be changed.

If there are circular connections in a system they are separated by consideration of a priority list.
