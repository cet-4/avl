Equation System

AMT Program enables to manage the engine and clutch torques during launch phases and shift events.

The AMT Program working procedure can be identified by looking at its output (in data bus) "AMT Status". It defines the following states:

  • status = 0 - idle phase.

    During this period usually the gearbox is in neutral state, the vehicle is standing still and the driver is holding the brake pedal.

  • status = 1 - launching phase.

    During this period the first gear is usually engaged and the driver applies a torque request via accelerator pedal to the AMT Program and therefore to the engine. The clutch will be controlled by the AMT Program in order to maintain the engine speed at the desired launch speed by means of the PI parameters (closed-loop control).

  • status = 2 - driving phase.

    During this period the driver controls the accelerator and brake pedal in order to follow the desired velocity profile. No particular control is acting in this phase.

  • status = 3 - upshift phase.

    During this time a request of gear change is sent to the AMT Program which acts on the following operation procedures:

    • status = 3.1 - slipping-opening phase.

      The clutch torque will be reduced to zero, via open loop, according to the maximum torque rate imposed by the user. The engine will be controlled via closed loop in order to maintain its speed constant.

    • status = 3.2 - synchronization phase.

      During this period the clutch will be completely open maintaining its zero torque; the engine will still be controlled imposing the acceleration value set up by the user for the upshift event and the new gear will be changed in the mechanical gearbox.

    • status = 3.3 - slipping-closing phase.

      During this phase the new gear has been engaged; the engine is decreasing its speed according to the acceleration target (like in point 3.2) and the clutch is being controlled in order to maintain the vehicle velocity constant.

    • status = 3.4 - end shift phase.

      Once the two clutch plates have the "same" speeds (clutch close to be in stick condition), the control of the clutch will impose to reach its maximum torque via open loop control and the engine will then be controlled by the torque request coming from the driver.

  • status = 4 - downshift phase.

    During this period a request of gear change is sent to the AMT Program which acts on the following operation procedures:

    • status = 4.1 - slipping-opening phase.

      See point 3.1.

    • status = 4.2 - synchronization phase.

      See point 3.2.

    • status = 4.3 - slipping-closing phase.

      See point 3.3, the engine speed in this case will be increased.

    • status = 4.4 - end shift phase.

      See point 3.4.

Due to the nature of the component, Quasi Stationary and Stationary solvers will not work properly and the simulation will not be allowed.
